## Affinity

[[Ancient Grudge]] and [[Troublemaker Ouphe]] supplement your removal suite, otherwise proceed with the normal game plan.

## Bogles

Not great, you have minimal enchantment removal. You'll need to race them with your glacially slow creature base.

## Broodscale

Your mainboard is well-positioned. Overload their combo protection with ample spot removal.

## Caw-Gates

Throw in some graveyard hate to supplement your vast arsenal of removal. [[Crypt Rats]] is sufficient with your beaters, there's no point trying to mow down every last [[Squadron Hawk]]

## Delver

[[Relic of Progenitus]] keeps their serpents expensive, and your value engine will outpace them as long as the removal keeps flowing. [[Defile]] is your weakest removal, as it can rarely kill their larger threats until late game.

## Elves

[[Crypt Rats]] and sweepers, as if there wasn't enough creature hate. This is a terrible matchup for the forest friends.

## Ephemerate

A little bit of graveyard hate slows their endgame value engine, though you should be able to easily take over before then with [[Writhing Chrysalis]]. Killing an [[Ephemerate]] target is also quite lucrative.

## Glintblade

Artifact hate wrecks their manabase, but it's not reliable as a game plan. Their fliers will take back the Monarch and Initiative from you easily, so use removal aggressively to clear the way for [[Thorn of the Black Rose]] and [[Avenging Hunter]].

## Madness Burn

Lifegain all day. Sit around your [[Campfire]] and summon your finishers.

## Ramp

They will get bigger creatures online faster than you, but you have the far superior removal suite. Stay alive and pick off their [[Writhing Chrysalis]] to let yours dominate. Slow down their enchantment ramp with [[Troublemaker Ouphe]].

## Skred

They have a similar game plan to yours, removal backed by Monarch enablers. Both of you will kill every creature on the board, so unfortunately their card selection and counters will pull ahead in securing the Monarch. Squeeze the most out of your [[Deadly Dispute]] + token engine.

## Synthesizer

Use every trick to stay alive. Monarch and Initiative are dangerous to use against a wide board, rely on sweepers and [[Writhing Chrysalis]] to stabilize first.

## Turbo Fog

This is a weird one. Either use repeated huge [[Crypt Rats]] activations to burn them out, or hate out their [[Stream of Thought]] for a decking win, or kill with repeated Initiative Trap! triggers.

## White Weenie

Bury them with removal and [[Writhing Chrysalis]]. [[Breath Weapon]] must play around [[Prismatic Strands]], if they have not boarded it out. [[Tithing Blade / Consuming Sepulcher]] sacrifice is not useful against their wide board, and the flip is too slow to close games compared to the Initiative.
